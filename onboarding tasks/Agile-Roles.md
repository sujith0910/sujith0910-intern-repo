# Roles in an Agile Team

## Key Roles in an Agile Team

### Developers

Developers are responsible for building features, fixing bugs, writing code, and maintaining the application. They work closely with other team members to ensure the product functions correctly and meets requirements.

### QA (Quality Assurance)

QA team members test the application to identify bugs, verify functionality, and ensure the software works as expected before release. They help improve software quality and reliability.

### Product Manager (PM)

The Product Manager focuses on understanding business requirements and user needs. They prioritise tasks, define features, and help guide the overall direction of the product.

### Agile Project Manager (Agile PM)

The Agile Project Manager helps organise workflows, remove blockers, and ensure the team follows Agile practices effectively. They support communication and coordination within the team.

### Other Supporting Roles

Other important roles include UX designers, stakeholders, and customer support teams. UX designers improve the user experience and interface design, stakeholders provide business direction and feedback, and customer support teams communicate user issues and suggestions back to the development team.

---

## Collaboration in Kanban vs Scrum

In Scrum, the team usually works in fixed sprints with regular meetings such as sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives. Team members collaborate closely during each sprint to complete planned work.

In Kanban, work moves continuously through different stages using a task board. The focus is more on managing workflow and limiting work in progress instead of working within fixed sprint timelines.

Although the workflows are slightly different, collaboration between developers, QA, product managers, and other team members remains important in both approaches.

## Reflection

### My Role in the Agile Team

As a Frontend Developer Intern, my role is to contribute to development tasks, learn team workflows, and support the implementation and testing of features. I also participate in understanding project requirements and improving my technical skills while working with the team.

My role contributes to the Agile process by helping complete assigned tasks, communicating progress, and collaborating with other team members during development and testing activities.

### Interaction with Other Roles

My responsibilities often overlap with QA and other developers because testing and reviewing features require teamwork. I also interact with supervisors to better understand feature requirements and expected behaviour.

One role I would frequently collaborate with is QA. Good communication with QA helps identify issues early and improves the overall quality of the application. I can improve teamwork by communicating more clearly about implemented features, discussing possible edge cases, and responding quickly to testing feedback.
