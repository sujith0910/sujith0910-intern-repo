# Data Privacy Reflection

## What types of data are considered confidential at Focus Bear?

Confidential data can include user account information, productivity and behavioural data, internal company discussions, project details, credentials, and any non-public business information shared within the company.

---

## What are best practices for handling confidential data?

Best practices include using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, avoiding sharing sensitive information through unsecured channels, and only accessing data required for assigned tasks. It is also important to lock devices when away and avoid storing confidential information in unsafe locations.

---

## How should you respond to a suspected data breach or accidental disclosure of confidential information?

If a data breach or accidental disclosure is suspected, it should be reported immediately to the appropriate team or supervisor. It is important not to hide the issue and to act quickly so the impact can be reduced as much as possible.

---

# Reflection

## What steps can you take to ensure you handle data securely in your daily tasks?

I can use secure passwords, avoid sharing confidential information unnecessarily, double-check before sending files or messages, and make sure my devices and accounts are protected.

---

## How should you store, share, and dispose of sensitive information safely?

Sensitive information should only be stored in secure and authorised systems. It should be shared only with the right people when necessary and removed safely when no longer needed.

---

## What are some common mistakes that lead to data privacy issues, and how can they be avoided?

Common mistakes include weak passwords, accidentally sharing information with the wrong person, using unsecured devices or networks, and failing to verify links or emails. These issues can be avoided by being careful, following security guidelines, and reviewing information before sharing it.

---

# Security Habit I Will Adopt

One habit I will adopt is regularly checking that sensitive files, accounts, and communication channels are secure before sharing or accessing information.

---

# Key Learning or security measure

A key learning for me is that data privacy is not only the responsibility of security teams. Every individual working with company or user information plays an important role in protecting privacy and maintaining trust.
