# Research & Learn

## What are examples of professional vs. unprofessional behaviour in a casual work environment?

Professional behaviour means being respectful, communicating clearly, attending meetings on time, and supporting teammates whenever needed. Unprofessional behaviour includes rude communication, ignoring messages, interrupting others, or not taking responsibility for assigned work.

## What does respectful communication look like in remote teams?

Respectful communication in remote teams means using a polite and clear tone, listening to others, responding professionally, and handling disagreements calmly without creating conflict.

## How can you give and receive feedback professionally?

Feedback should focus on improving the work rather than criticizing the person. When receiving feedback, it is important to stay open-minded, listen carefully, and use it as an opportunity to learn and improve.

# Reflection

## Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?

Yes, during both academic projects and work experience, I have seen good teamwork where everyone communicated openly, respected each other’s ideas, and helped each other when needed. Clear communication and accountability made the teamwork effective.

## What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?

I can communicate clearly, maintain a respectful tone, actively listen during discussions, and ask questions whenever clarification is needed instead of making assumptions.

## How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?

I can contribute by being approachable, supportive, respectful, and maintaining a positive attitude while working with the team.

## How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?

I can prepare by reviewing my tasks beforehand, preparing updates or questions, and joining meetings on time so discussions remain productive.

## What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?

Being proactive means taking initiative, regularly updating progress, asking questions when unsure, and trying to solve problems independently before seeking help.

## How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?

I would first send a polite follow-up message after waiting a reasonable amount of time. If the delay starts affecting deadlines or work progress, I would respectfully escalate the issue to the appropriate person.

## What are some things you won't do?

I will avoid disrespectful communication, ignoring feedback, creating negativity in the team, or missing deadlines without proper communication.
