# Competitive Landscape

## What makes Focus Bear different from these apps?

After looking at different productivity apps, I noticed that Focus Bear is not only about blocking distractions. Apps like Freedom and Cold Turkey mainly focus on restricting websites and apps, while Tiimo and Routinery are more focused on routines and scheduling. Focus Bear combines both focus management and habit-building, which makes it feel more like a daily productivity companion instead of just a blocking tool.

## If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?

As a user, I would choose Focus Bear because it encourages consistency rather than only forcing restrictions. The combination of routines, focus sessions, and distraction blocking feels more balanced and practical for long-term productivity. It also seems useful for building better daily habits instead of only preventing distractions.

## What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t?

One feature I noticed in some competitor apps, especially Tiimo, is a more visual and interactive daily schedule layout. The visual timeline and calendar-style structure can make task planning easier and more engaging for users.

## Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?

One improvement Focus Bear could make is adding a more detailed visual dashboard for daily planning and progress tracking. This could help users quickly understand how they are spending their time and make routines feel more interactive and motivating.

## Honest Reflection

From my research, I feel Focus Bear has a unique approach because it combines focus tools with routines and habit support. At the same time, other apps also do certain things very well. Freedom is strong for distraction blocking across devices, Cold Turkey is known for strict blocking, and Tiimo stands out with visual organisation features. I think Focus Bear already has a good foundation, but improving the planning and visual experience could make it even stronger.
