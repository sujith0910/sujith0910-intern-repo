# Cyber Security Guidelines

## 1. Common threats in a remote work setup
In a remote work environment, there are several common security risks that can affect both personal and company data. These include phishing emails that try to trick users into sharing credentials, weak or reused passwords, and unsafe public Wi-Fi connections. There is also a risk of downloading malicious files or clicking suspicious links, which can lead to malware infections.

---

## 2. Best practices for keeping devices and accounts secure
Keeping devices and accounts secure requires a few consistent habits. It is important to use strong and unique passwords for different accounts and keep systems updated regularly. Avoid installing software from unknown sources and be cautious when accessing work-related data on public networks. Even simple actions like locking your device can make a big difference.

---

## 3. Why locking your computer is important
Locking your computer when stepping away is a simple but essential habit. It prevents unauthorized access to your files and protects sensitive information. Even if you are away for a short time, leaving your system unlocked can expose important data to risk.

---

## 4. Handling phishing and suspicious links
Phishing attempts are becoming more common and harder to identify. It is important to always check the sender’s email address and avoid clicking on unexpected links or downloading unknown attachments. If something feels suspicious, it is safer to ignore or report it rather than taking a risk.

---

## 5. Strong passwords and password manager
A strong password usually includes a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. It should not be based on personal information or common words. Using the same password across multiple accounts increases risk. A password manager can help generate and store secure passwords, making it easier to maintain good security practices.

---

## 6. Importance of 2FA
Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security to accounts. Even if a password is compromised, 2FA helps prevent unauthorized access. It is especially important to enable 2FA for accounts like email, GitHub, and any work-related platforms.

---
